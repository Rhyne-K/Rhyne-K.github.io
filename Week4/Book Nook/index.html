A small, cozy spot dedicated to my favorite books and genres.

FICTION

Wuthering Heights by Emily Brontë

A haunting Gothic masterpiece of obsession and revenge set against the windswept Yorkshire moors. It follows the destructive love between Heathcliff and Catherine Earnshaw, a bond so intense it transcends death and haunts the lives of two generations.

NON-FICTION

True Love by Thich Nhat Hanh

Thich Nhat Hanh defines true love through the four elements of loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and inclusiveness. The book offers practical mindfulness meditations to help practitioners be truly present for themselves and their partners.

SCI-FI

Dune by Frank Herbet

An epic of world-building where politics, religion, and ecology intersect on a desert planet. The story follows Paul Atreides as he navigates a deadly power struggle for the "spice," rising as a messianic leader among the desert-dwelling Fremen to reclaim his family’s legacy.

FANTASY

The Tolkien Series by J.R.R. Tolkein

The ultimate journey of courage and fellowship. It follows a band of heroes, led by the humble hobbit Frodo Baggins, on a perilous quest across Middle-earth to destroy the One Ring and defeat the dark lord Sauron.

GOTHIC HORROR

The Complete Tales and Poems of Edgar Allan Poe

Edgar Allan Poe’s collection of tales and poems blending Gothic horror, mystery, and melancholy. His work often explores the dark side of the human psyche, featuring themes of premature burial, madness, and the loss of a beautiful woman.

CLASSICS

The Odyssey by Homer

The Odyssey follows the Greek hero Odysseus on a grueling ten-year journey home to Ithaca after the Trojan War. While he battles monsters, gods, and temptations at sea, his wife Penelope fends off aggressive suitors at home.
